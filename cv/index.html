Professional Summary

I work at the intersection of rigorous machine learning research and high-stakes system engineering. I studied computer science at MIT and am a founder in the Y Combinator S26 batch, where I focus on translating deep technical ideas into real world systems. My research spans MIT CSAIL, the Media Lab, and LIDS, focusing on distribution shifts in computer vision, human computer interaction, multimodal data analysis, and multiagent strategy with LLMs. Through engineering work at Amazon and early-stage startups, I’ve explored human in the loop design for LLM-based systems and how to orchestrate agents to produce efficient, rigorous, and interpretable outcomes. I’m particularly interested in building intelligent systems to solve complex problems, combining advances in machine learning with a nuanced understanding of human behavior and strategic decision making.
