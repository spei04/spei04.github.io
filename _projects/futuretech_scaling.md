**MIT CSAIL FutureTech Lab** — Mar 2024 – May 2025 · Advised by Dr. Wensu Li

Analyzed the economics of AI labor automation and fit functional forms of computer-vision model scaling laws.

- **Scaling laws** — fit functional forms of fine-tuning scaling laws for computer-vision models; wrote 1,000+ lines of Python to run experiments, visualize data, and analyze survey results
- **Labor automation** — used GPT-4o prompt engineering to produce robust estimates of classification complexity for automating occupations; ran a case study and demographic analysis across occupations, and used LLM APIs to cluster occupations and assess grouping robustness
- Conducted literature reviews across 30+ scholarly articles on information theory, LLM benchmarks, and AI labor augmentation
- **Shopping analysis** — 500+ lines of R using logistic regression to estimate customer preferences for online vs. offline shopping across factual, fitting, transaction, and acquisition phases
- Monitored deployment of a Qualtrics survey measuring the effectiveness of LLM-generated outputs
