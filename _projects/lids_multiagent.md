**MIT Laboratory for Information & Decision Systems (LIDS)** — Feb 2026 – Jun 2026 · with Kate Donahue and Manish Raghavan

Built a scalable framework to study how large language models generate, follow, and evolve strategies in multi-agent games.

- Implemented **systematic simulations across multiple LLMs** to evaluate strategic behavior
- Identified model-dependent strategic tendencies and linked behavioral differences to training and architectural choices
- Applied statistical analysis to uncover patterns relevant to **AI safety, coordination, and incentive alignment**
- Delivered a reproducible codebase enabling further analysis of LLMs in strategic environments
