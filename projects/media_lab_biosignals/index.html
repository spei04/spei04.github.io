Multimodal Biosignal Processing for HCI

MIT Media Lab, Fluid Interfaces (Nataliya Kosmyna) · EEG/EMG/EOG for ASD communication and interoception

MIT Media Lab — Fluid Interfaces Lab — Feb 2024 – May 2026 · Advised by Nataliya Kosmyna

Research on biosignal processing (EEG, EMG, EOG), human-computer interaction, and adaptive AI systems across several publications.

  • First author — Exploring Motor Patterns in Minimally Verbal ASD Through Surface Electromyography: in partnership with Massachusetts General Hospital, built a pipeline applying ML models for subject classification and test-score regression from EMG eigenvalue features; wrote 5,000+ lines of Python for preprocessing, feature extraction, and analysis (code)
  • Co-author — IEEE BSN 2024: “Privacy-Preserving Eye Movement Classification with Camera-Free, Non-Invasive EOG-EEG Glasses” — bandpass-filtered EEG/EOG signals and trained ML models to predict eye blink/gaze movement (1,000+ lines of Python) (code)
  • Co-author — ACM IMWUT 2025: “Exploring the Emotional Effects of Enhanced Interoception via Heartbeat-Synchronized Haptic Feedback” — analyzed emotion modulation with Fitbit heart-rate feedback and evocative video clips; 3,000+ lines of Python for statistical analysis of valence/arousal and emotional disturbance (code)
  • Co-author — Validating Mobile Brain-Sensing System for Real-World Neurophysiological Assessment: analyzed EEG/EOG results for diagnosing minimally verbal ASD, with Welch t-tests and correlation significance testing (paper)
  • Co-author — NeuroChat (submitted to CHI 2025): customizing AI learning by adapting GPT-generated questions in real time from EEG signals during learning
  • Multimodal study — combined EOG/EEG/EMG with feature- and modal-level attention fusion; compared accuracies across logistic regression, random forest, gradient boosting, KNN, and SVM
  • In-the-wild interoception study — developed and deployed Qualtrics screening and daily/weekly check-in surveys to study interoception’s effect on mental wellbeing
