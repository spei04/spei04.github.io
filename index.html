I thrive at the intersection of rigorous machine learning research and high-stakes system engineering. As an MIT Computer Science and Engineering student and YC Founder (S26), my work is driven by the belief that the most profound breakthroughs in AI are only as impactful as the systems that deliver them.

My research background spans CSAIL (Vision Lab, advised by Prof. Sara Beery), the MIT Media Lab (Fluid Interfaces group), and LIDS (Laboratory for Information & Decision Systems). I have focused on distribution shifts and diffusion-based data augmentation, multi-modal biosignal processing (EEG, EMG, EOG), and the reasoning capabilities of large language models.

At Palette (YC S26), I am synthesizing these interests to build the next generation of intelligent tools for the modern workforce — architecting the core ML inference pipeline and full-stack product infrastructure from zero to production.

Previously, I interned at Amazon (PXT) building an LLM-powered RAG feedback pipeline and AWS CDK infrastructure, and conducted research at A*STAR Singapore on deep learning for histomorphology in cancer tissue images.

  2. ICML
    PerplexMATH: Steering LLMs Towards Mathematical Reasoning
    Serena Pei and others
    In ICML 2025 NewInML Workshop, 2025
    ICML 2025 Abs Bib HTML Code

    Accepted poster and presentation at ICML 2025 NewInML Workshop

    Large language models have demonstrated unprecedented progress in reasoning abilities, yet performance degrades on hard problems and is brittle under structural perturbations. This study investigates whether LLMs balance memorization and reasoning when solving hard math problems. Through systematic evaluation we find a computation-reasoning trade-off: models solve computationally intensive problems faster but with 50% less engagement in some models, suggesting heuristic shortcuts under load. We introduce PerplexMATH, a hard math problem dataset with diverse perturbation types and a reasoning framework for evaluating LLMs with more robust, human-like reasoning capabilities.
